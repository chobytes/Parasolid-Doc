PK_LATTICE_make_patterned    

PK_ERROR_code_t                      PK_LATTICE_make_patterned
(
--- received arguments ---
PK_LATTICE_t                         lattice,      --- core lattice
const PK_LATTICE_make_patterned_o_t *options,      --- options

--- returned arguments ---
PK_LATTICE_make_patterned_r_t *const results       --- results
)


This function creates a lattice made of patterned repetitions of the given
lattice.


Specific Errors:
    PK_ERROR_size_mismatch          Matched lrods or lballs have different
                                    radii. (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_core_breach            The core encroaches on cells beyond its
                                    direct adjacencies. (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_no_lattice             No resultant lattice would be produced.
                                    (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_invalid_geometry       A lattice with invalid geometry has been
                                    provided or would be produced. (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_pattern_failed         Failed to create a patterned lattice.
                                    (MILD/SERIOUS)


This function creates a lattice comprised of repetitions of the given
'lattice' in the form of the given pattern.

The 'cell' tessellates to create a pattern of cells containing copies
of the core 'lattice' whose union forms the result lattice. The 'bounds'
determines the repetitions of the 'cell'.

Optionally a 'form' and/or 'callback' may be supplied to relocate and deform
the pattern. If both a callback and a 'form' are supplied, the callback is
applied after any deformation from the 'form'. If both a callback and a
rectilinear or original 'form' are supplied, the callback positions are applied
in the 3D world coordinate space. If both a callback and an axial 'form' are
supplied, the callback is applied against the positions in the context of the
parameter space formed by the pattern's 'axis' and 'origin' (see documentation
of PK_pattern_axial_o_t for more detail).

If the 'trim_type' in the 'bound' is set to PK_pattern_trim_type_exclude_c,
any cell whose projected ltopol positions do not lie entirely within the
constraints of the 'trim_curves' will be excluded from the pattern.
If the 'trim_type' in the 'bound' is set to PK_pattern_trim_type_partial_c,
any cell whose projected ltopol positions do not lie entirely within the
constraints of the 'trim_curves' will be clipped at its boundary, using the
options provided in 'clip'. In either case, this operation is performed after
any deformation that is to be applied from the 'form', but before the
deformation from the 'callback'.

Matches between adjacent repetitions are defined by coincident lballs and
lrods.
