PK_BODY_make_patterned    

PK_ERROR_code_t                   PK_BODY_make_patterned
(
--- received arguments ---
PK_BODY_t                         body,         --- core body
double                            tolerance,    --- tolerance
const PK_BODY_make_patterned_o_t *options,      --- options

--- returned arguments ---
PK_BODY_make_patterned_r_t *const results       --- results
)


This function creates a body made of patterned repetitions of the given body.


Specific Errors:
    PK_ERROR_unsuitable_entity      Supplied 'body' has classic geometry.
                                    (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_bad_type               The type of supplied 'body' is neither
                                    sheet nor solid.
                                    (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_bad_mvx_normals        Supplied 'body' has a mesh with stored
                                    mvertex normals.
                                    (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_vectors_coplanar       Either the supplied 'cell' or the
                                    rectilinear pattern are tolerantly flat.
                                    (MILD)
    PK_ERROR_too_many_facets        Geometry on result would have too many
                                    facets.
                                    (SERIOUS)
    PK_ERROR_pattern_failed         Failed to create a patterned body.
                                    (MILD/SERIOUS)
    PK_ERROR_no_body                No resultant body would be produced.
                                    (MILD)



This function creates a body comprised of repetitions of the given 'body' in
the form of the given pattern.

The 'cell' tessellates to create a pattern of cells containing copies of the
core 'body'. The 'bounds' determines the number of repetitions of the
'cell'.

Optionally a 'form' and/or 'callback' may be supplied to relocate and deform
the pattern. If both a callback and a 'form' are supplied, the callback is
applied after any deformation from the 'form'. If both a callback and a
rectilinear or original 'form' are supplied, the callback positions are applied
in the 3D world coordinate space. If both a callback and an axial 'form' are
supplied, the callback is applied against the positions in the context of the
parameter space formed by the pattern's 'axis' and 'origin' (see documentation
of PK_pattern_axial_o_t for more detail).

If the 'trim_type' in the 'bound' is set to PK_pattern_trim_type_exclude_c,
any cell whose projected mvertex positions do not lie entirely within the 2d
planar constraints of the 'trim_curves' will be excluded from the pattern.
If the 'trim_type' in the 'bound' is set to PK_pattern_trim_type_partial_c,
any cell whose projected mvertex positions do not lie entirely within the 2d
planar constraints of the 'trim_curves' will be sectioned at its boundary.
In either case, this operation is applied after the deformation from the
'form', but before the deformation from the 'callback'.

Matches between adjacent repetitions are defined by tolerantly coincident
topologies.

'body' must have only facet geometry.

The resulting body will have as little topology as possible, and may be
disjoint.
