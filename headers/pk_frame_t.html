PK_FRAME_t    



This type represents a FRAME.  A frame is a boundary that divides its owning
topology.

Used in:

PK_BODY_ask_frames
PK_FRAME_ask_body
PK_FRAME_ask_geometry
PK_FRAME_ask_owner
PK_FRAME_ask_sense
PK_FRAME_reverse
PK_LATTICE_ask_regions_r_t
PK_REGION_ask_lattices_r_t
PK_REGION_embed_lattices_r_t
PK_TOPOL_find_frames_r_t


A frame is a type of topology (PK_CLASS_frame is a subclass of
PK_CLASS_topol).

A frame owns a single geometry, and is owned by a single topology.  A topology
may own multiple frames.  A single geometry may be owned by multiple frames
(these frames must be owned by distinct topologies in the same body).

Currently FRAMEs may only own LATTICEs and be owned by REGIONs.

Currently a REGION may only own a single FRAME.
