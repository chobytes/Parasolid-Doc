struct PK_pattern_axial_o_s
    {
    int             o_t_version;    --- version number of structure
    PK_AXIS1_sf_t   axis;           --- axis of pattern
                                    --- (location 0,0,0 direction 0,1,0)
    PK_VECTOR_t     origin;         --- origin of pattern (0, 0, 0)
    double          radial_length;  --- length in the radial direction (0)
    double          axial_length;   --- length parallel to the axis (0)
    int             reps_per_period;--- number of repetitions per complete
                                    --- period (0)
    };
typedef struct PK_pattern_axial_o_s PK_pattern_axial_o_t;




This structure contains information defining an axial pattern.


Specific Errors:
PK_ERROR_radius_eq_0              'origin' lies within session precision
                                  of 'axis' (MILD)

PK_ERROR_bad_value                'reps_per_period' is zero or negative
                                  (MILD)

PK_ERROR_distance_le_0            'radial_length' or 'axial_length' is zero
                                  or negative (MILD)

Used in:

PK_pattern_form_t


Description of fields:


'axis'                      Axis of pattern


'origin'                    Origin of pattern, defining and lying coincident
                            to its parametric seam.


'radial_length'             Length between adjacent repetitions in the radial
                            direction


'axial_length'              Length between adjacent repetitions in the
                            direction of the axis


'reps_per_period'           Number of repetitions that would form a full period
                            around the axis (2pi)

A transform will be applied to ensure the minimum corner of the 'skewbox'
of the 'cell' will be mapped to the 'origin' of the axial pattern, along with
the following direction mappings -
 o cell's i_axis -> around the form's axis (positive is anti-clockwise when
                    looking down the axis)
 o cell's j_axis -> along the form's axis
 o cell's k_axis -> radial direction

The number of repetitions in each direction is controlled by the 'bound'
structure based on this mapping -
 o 'repetitions_i' is the number of repetitions in the arc around the axis. It
   must be no greater than 'reps_per_period'.
 o 'repetitions_j' is the number of repetitions in the direction of the axis.
 o 'repetitions_k' is the number of repetitions in the radial direction.
In order to restrict the pattern to fewer than three dimensions, the first and
second value of the 'bound' structure's corresponding repetitions should be
equal, for example [0, 0].

The pattern's 'axis' and 'origin' form a parameter space which has the
following mappings -
 o The first coordinate is the u parameter (around the form's axis).
 o The second coordinate is the v parameter (along the form's axis).
 o The third coordinate is the radial direction.
The u,v parameters are equivalent to those of a cylinder; see PK_CYL_sf_t for
details. The pattern's 'origin' has the parameter space coordinates [0, 0, 0].
The lengths in the radial and axial directions are parameterised so that they
represent model space distance.

If the axial pattern is used in combination with a 'callback', the positions
supplied to the 'callback' will be in the context of the pattern's parameter
space. The u parameter values will be supplied in the range [0, 2pi].

If the axial pattern is used in combination with 'bound' 'trim_curves', the
polyline positions must be supplied in the context of the pattern's parameter
space. The 'trim_curves' bound the pattern in the ith direction (around the
form's axis) and jth direction (along the form's axis). The polyline positions
must lie on the cylinder surface of the pattern's parameter space, so their
kth direction coordinate (radial direction) must have a value of 0.
