PK_REGION_remove_lattice    

PK_ERROR_code_t                     PK_REGION_remove_lattice
(
--- received arguments ---
PK_REGION_t                         region,      --- region
PK_LATTICE_t                        lattice,     --- lattice to remove
const PK_REGION_remove_lattice_o_t *options,     --- options structure

--- returned arguments ---
PK_REGION_remove_lattice_r_t *const results      --- results structure
)


This function removes the given 'lattice' from the given 'region'.


Specific Errors:
    PK_ERROR_not_connected          The 'lattice' is not embedded in 'region'
                                    (MILD).
    PK_ERROR_wrong_entity           An attribute selected to be transferred
                                    to 'region' may not be owned by a region or
                                    is of class 2, 3, or 5. (MILD).


Embedded lattices are attached to frames, which are attached to regions.  When
the 'lattice' is removed, it is detached from the frame and the frame is
deleted. If the 'lattice' is no longer owned by any frames it will become
orphan geometry of its partition.

Upon deletion of the last lattice in 'region', if 'region' is void, it will
be made solid, as the material in the region is now only bounded by the shells
of the region.

If as a result of removing the 'lattice', the 'region' is changed from void
to solid and the body is then a manifold solid, the body type will change to
PK_BODY_type_solid_c.

If the 'region' has no density attribute (SDL/TYSA_REGION_DENSITY) prior to
lattice removal, and the frame has a frame density attribute, then the
frame density (SDL/TYSA_FRAME_DENSITY) will be converted to a region density
which will then be attached to the 'region'. If both the 'region' and the frame
possess density attributes prior to lattice removal, the 'region' density
will remain unchanged and the frame density will be deleted along with
the frame itself.  If the 'region' has a region density attribute and the frame
has no density attribute then the 'region' density shall be left unchanged.

Optionally, additional attributes that may be owned by both regions and frames
can be transferred to the 'region' upon removing a lattice. See the options
structure for further details.

Optionally, the user may specify whether the 'region' should replace the
deleted frame in any groups where it is a member. See the options
structure for further details.
